Eurasian Tree Sparrow

Also known as: Passer montanus

Description
Size 12.5-14cm
(Roughly 10% smaller than the house sparrow)
Wingspan About 21cm
Weight 18-28g
In late April 1870, European birds were imported from Germany to St. Louis, Missouri, to provide them for European immigrants. Eurasian Tree Sparrows were included in the shipment. After their arrival, they were spread from northeastern Missouri to west-central Illinois and southeastern Iowa.

Appearance

Its color of crown and nape are rich chestnut, and its ears are kidney-shaped black. Its beak is blue in the summer, becoming black in the winter and under its chin and throat are also black. Its wings are mixed light and brown and there are 2 white bars on them. There is no difference between males and females such as appearance, plumage. The juveniles look-alike to the adults, though the color of their plumage is a blur. From autumn to winter, they gain their weight despite decreasing their stored fat. The change is because of accelerating feather growth and containing water in the body.

Food

They search for food on the ground singly or in flocks. They eat seeds, grain, or insects and sometimes they pick up food from trees or grasses.

Courtship behavior

In spring and fall, males appeal to females by chirping and flapping their wings. Their chirp sound is used to socialize and attract females.

Habitat

They are living in open, wooded places such as villages, parks and farmland, but some species such as house sparrows are living in the human habitations.

Population

Those birds from Germany have more syllable types and less structure in the population than the US birds. Their population in North America is small and it caused a loss of genetic diversity. For the most part of their habitat is in Europe and some countries in Asia. As you can see from their name, they live on Eurasian Continent. According to iNaturalist, they are not living near Toronto.

Family

It is believed that its origin is in Africa. Studies say that Eurasian tree sparrows branched from the other Eurasian members of its genus early, to plain-backed and Spanish sparrows. Eurasian tree sparrow and American tree sparrow are not related, they are in a different family.

Origin of name

The name, Eurasian tree sparrow, comes from two Latin words: passer as “sparrow” and montanus as “of the mountains”. The name is given because they prefer to nest in the holes in trees. They are distributed in the east of the US but not that many.

Relationship with humans

In some countries, the Eurasian tree sparrow is classified as a pest. They eat cereal and fruits from fields and fruit farms and their dropping makes the grains spoiled. In Australia, it is forbidden to bring this species into Western Australia by quarantine rules. In China, the government tried to decrease the damage of crops by Eurasian tree sparrows, by placing many scarecrows and it drove the birds to death. However, by doing so, the number of insect pests increased because those insects are usually consumed by the birds. As a result, this “Four pests campaign” caused a famine and over 30 million people died between 1959 and 1961.

Fact

  • Eurasian tree sparrows typically live near the human habitat, but it has been moved from urban area to rural area because they are larger and more aggressive than House sparrows.
  • Eurasian tree sparrows vary in its plumage and size and it has been distinguished into 33 subspecies outside of North America. The North American birds are from the most widespread subspecies called ”montanus”.
  • The most long-lived Eurasian tree sparrow in North America was over 4 years old and it was recorded in Illinois in 1972, but the typical lifespan is about 3 years.
